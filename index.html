Software Developer Intern

Pacific Northwest Research Institute

I developed automation to sequence the destructive LINE-1 gene and analyze its impact and evolution across different ethnicities to help researchers understand it’s determinantal effects and future. I implemented this solution with Perl and Python using tools including BWA Tools, BLAST and BAMTOOLS. This experience taught me to how to apply my coding skills to solve problems in health care as well as exposed me to new computational tools. I also learnt how to persevere to find creative solutions and quickly adapt to new technologies and toolsets.

Software Developer and Research Assistant

University of Washington, GRID Lab for Neuroengineering

I created a software interface that is integrated with epilepsy patients to monitor their neural response and understand their varied brain activity while playing a custom VR game. I implemented this using an OpenXR API layer with C++ that tracks patients’ VR controller and headset position information. This information is then analyzed to create patterns of brain activity. I presented my work at the University of Washington’s Research Symposium. This being my first advanced research project, I learnt to take an ambiguous problem and methodically work through layers of problem-solving to arrive at the full solution in the end.
